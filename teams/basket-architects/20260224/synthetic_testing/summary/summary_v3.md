The V3 results reveal a clear "have your cake and eat it too" opportunity:

1. **Restore the honesty line for journal_graveyard.** "Most apps like this die after a week. We know that." was load-bearing. Put it back in the retention section. This is a targeted restore, not a length increase â€” the line replaces the current opening of the retention section.

2. **Restore the specific examples for taste_curator.** "Your 8/10 Thai place in the East Village. Your 4/10 tourist trap in Miami." These grounded the concept. Add them back as a single line in the solution section.

3. **Expand the cold-start explanation slightly for city_explorer.** The one-liner is punchy but the V2 explanatory version (3-4 sentences) converted at 92%. Find a middle ground â€” 2 sentences of context around "first it's your map."

4. **Keep the tighter problem section.** The merged fomo_scroller paragraph at its current length is driving the best fomo_scroller results yet (+25pp conversion). Don't expand this back.

5. **Keep annual pricing.** The $99/year option is the highest-impact pricing change across all rounds.

**Strategy: Selective restoration.** V4 should be V3's length with V2's high-performing content surgically restored where the data says it matters. Net length change should be ~5% longer than V3 (not back to V2 length).
