## What Moved the Needle

### Hero reframe (+50pp for routine_breaker conversion)
"Stop ending up at the same place again" directly replaced the "scrolling six apps" framing that routine_breakers had rejected. Nearly every persona in this bucket cited the headline as their strongest hook. The conversion gain (+50pp) is the single biggest shift of the round. The hypothesis was right: their problem is low motivation to try something new, not research fatigue.

### Three example cards (culture_seeker: 50% â†’ 100% conversion)
Culture seekers went from 50% to 100% conversion. Every persona in this bucket cited the example shortlist as the turning point. ### "Real budgets" line (budget_conscious_socializer: 33% â†’ 83% conversion)
The single sentence â€” "We're building this for New York, which means we're building it for people with real budgets" â€” was cited as the strongest line by 5 of 12 budget personas. ### "Get early access" CTA framing
The passive-waitlist objection was almost entirely absent in V2. Multiple personas who flagged it in V1 either didn't mention it or gave it minimal attention. The reframing worked.

### "No account required to browse"
This one line reduced quiet_explorer dealbreakers from 25% to 0%. It didn't move conversion dramatically (still 42%) but it eliminated the hard-no responses. The remaining quiet_explorer resistance is trust/quality-based, not privacy-based â€” a solvable problem.
