## What Drove the V3 Gains

### "How We Decide" section (+42pp for quiet_explorer conversion)
The transparency section ‚Äî explaining sourcing (gallery mailing lists, community boards, organizer networks), quality bar (credibility + "would we go ourselves"), and personalization mechanism (skip/save signals, not social graph) ‚Äî was the single highest-leverage change in V3. It directly answered the objection that had persisted across V1 and V2: "this is a black box." The section is factual and specific rather than marketing language, which is exactly what high-skepticism personas need.

### New example cards (+33pp for routine_breaker, meaningful across all buckets)
Replacing mainstream examples with genuinely obscure ones ("Stitched Futures" Bronx textile show, Taiwanese pop-up dinner, Navy Yard site-specific theater with "Not on Eventbrite" label) addressed the culture_seeker and quiet_explorer critique that V2 examples were "proof of curation, not discovery." Multiple personas across buckets cited "Not on Eventbrite" as the strongest single proof point.

### "Free to get started" (+17pp for budget_conscious_socializer)
Stating the app pricing explicitly pushed budget_conscious_socializer from 83% to 100% conversion. 10 of 12 personas in this bucket had cited "is the app free?" as their #1 unanswered question in V2. One sentence removed the question entirely.

### 48-hour shortlist promise
Converted the "is this vaporware?" concern into a testable commitment. Specifically converted Ben Shapiro-Stein (quiet_explorer, skepticism 5) who had been unmoved by V1 and V2: "The 48-hour promise makes it real enough to try."

### "Cash bars, sales pitches, anything where you'll feel tricked"
Specific callout in the free events objection handler. Camila Herrera (budget_conscious_socializer): "They literally described my worst NYC event experience." Isabel Torres: directly addressed V2 objection that "parks aren't curated."

## Remaining Objections (V3)

None are dealbreakers. The residual objections are refinements, not blockers:

1. **"How We Decide" section is too long / interrupts page flow** ‚Äî 6+ overwhelmed_planner and routine_breaker personas. They didn't need it (already sold), but it slowed them down. For high-converting segments, the section adds friction rather than value.
2. **"Who are the curators?"** ‚Äî quiet_explorer and culture_seeker. Editorial team transparency ("who is 'we'?") is the last remaining trust gap.
3. **"Free tier scope"** ‚Äî budget_conscious_socializer (5/12). "Free to get started" is clear but "what does free get you vs paid?" is unanswered.
4. **Geographic scatter of example cards** ‚Äî routine_breaker and budget_conscious_socializer. The three example cards span Bronx, Crown Heights, Navy Yard. Doesn't reflect a realistic user's shortlist (which would be geographically filtered).
5. **Couple/date use case** ‚Äî 4 personas across buckets. Still invisible.
6. **Film events** ‚Äî Yara Nazari (culture_seeker, film editor). Absent from category list.

## Recommendations for V4

### Only worth doing if:
The goal of V4 should be testing whether the "How We Decide" section can be restructured to be faster for high-converting segments while remaining useful for skeptics ‚Äî and whether editorial transparency ("a small team of NYC-based curators") moves quiet_explorer from 83% to 90%+.

### Specific V4 changes to test:

1. **Collapse or shorten "How We Decide" for fast-converting segments.** Move the detailed explanation to a "How it works ‚Üí" expand link or a secondary FAQ section. Let the example cards carry the proof for overwhelmed_planners and routine_breakers; reserve the full section for skeptics. This addresses the page-flow critique from 6+ personas.

2. **Add one line of editorial transparency.** "A small team of NYC-based curators" or "curated by people who actually live here" directly addresses quiet_explorer's residual "who is 'we'?" concern. No team page needed ‚Äî just one line.

3. **Clarify free tier scope.** "Your full shortlist, free" or "no features locked behind a paywall to start" resolves the 5-persona budget ambiguity.

4. **Fix geographic scatter in example cards.** Show all three example cards as being from a single neighborhood (e.g., all Brooklyn, all Manhattan) to demonstrate that the proximity filter works. The current examples look like a "best of NYC" showcase, not a personalized shortlist.

### Lower priority:
- Add film events to category list (for Yara Nazari type)
- Add one couple/date framing line
- Solo-friendly example card
