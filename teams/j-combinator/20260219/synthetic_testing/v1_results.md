## Recommendations for V2

### High priority (biggest conversion impact):

1. **Add 2-3 more example cards** to the How It Works section. Show variety: a pop-up, a free outdoor event, a comedy show. This directly addresses culture_seeker skepticism and proves catalog depth.

2. **Make the budget filter a first-class feature**, not a sub-step. Consider a dedicated callout or mini-section: "Whether your budget is $0 or no limit" with a specific free example card. This makes budget_conscious_socializers feel seen.

3. **Add one line of algorithmic transparency** to the "How do you know what I'll actually like?" objection. Something like: "Based on what you pick, save, and skip â€” not your social media data." This addresses quiet_explorer's black-box concern without overexplaining.

### Medium priority:

4. **Add a group planning mention.** Even a single line: "Planning for friends? Share your shortlist via text." This addresses the #2 objection.

5. **Reframe the hero for routine_breakers.** The current hero targets research fatigue. Consider testing a secondary headline that targets novelty: "Your next favorite thing in NYC â€” found in 30 seconds."

6. **Replace or supplement social proof quotes.** Current quotes are interview-sourced (they describe the problem, not the product). As the product develops, one beta tester quote would dramatically increase trust.

### Lower priority (V3+):

7. **Add a launch timeline** to the CTA. "Early access opens soon" is vague. Even "Spring 2026" is better.

8. **Test CTA copy.** "Get your first shortlist" may convert better than "Join the waitlist" â€” it's more concrete and implies immediate value.

9. **Address quiet_explorer privacy concerns.** A single line: "No account required. No data sold. No social profile linked." could reduce their 50% dealbreaker rate.
